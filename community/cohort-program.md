# OpenQuant cohort program (F5.5)

A 6-week, async-first cohort that turns Python-literate traders and
students into contributors. Borrowed structure from the Zen Hacks playbook.

## Cohort shape

- **Size:** 25 participants (cap hard; quality > scale)
- **Cadence:** 6 weeks, ~5 hours/week
- **Format:** async work + weekly live office hours (Fri 19:00 IST)
- **Cost:** free; selective application
- **Outcome to graduate:** one merged PR + one passing zoo strategy
  (educational allowed) + one public writeup

## Weekly arc

| Week | Topic | Output |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1 | Setup, manifesto, 60-second quickstart | `pip install` + first tearsheet |
| 2 | Data integrity: bhavcopy, corporate actions, HDFC | annotated notebook |
| 3 | Honest costs: STT, brokerage, slippage, taxes | gross vs net teardown of one famous strategy |
| 4 | Backtesting discipline: walk-forward, no peeking | strategy + walk-forward report |
| 5 | Compliance: SEBI 2026, kill switch, paper engine | paper run of cohort strategy |
| 6 | Ship: merge PR + zoo entry + writeup | merged PR + post in `#showcase` |

## Selection rubric

1. Can run Python on their own machine (screenshot of `python -V`).
2. One paragraph: an honest backtest they're embarrassed by, and why.
3. Commits to all 6 weeks.

Anti-criteria (auto-decline):

- "I want to learn algo trading to make ₹X/month."
- Anything resembling tips, signals, or RIA-adjacent language.

## Maintainer commitments

- Two maintainers per cohort, rotating.
- Office hours recorded and posted publicly the next day.
- Every PR from a cohort gets a review SLA of 48h.

## Distribution channels for sign-up

1. Existing 1,500+ community + GDG chapters
2. College CS societies (NIT/IIT/IIIT clubs)
3. X + LinkedIn launch posts (one cohort per quarter)
4. `#welcome` and `#introductions` on the Discord

## Success metric

- ≥70% completion rate
- ≥1 cohort grad becomes a recurring contributor
- ≥3 strategies merged to `oq-zoo` per cohort (educational counts)
